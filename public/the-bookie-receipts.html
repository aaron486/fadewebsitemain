01

The Idea

In betting, "show me the receipts" means one thing: prove your record. For forty years Sal kept his receipts in the back room — every bet, every result — where nobody could see them but him. That secrecy was the bookie's edge.

Now he's pinning them up in the open, delighted, under the FADE logo. Because the game changed: on FADE your record is public, verified, out where everyone can see it. The corkboard is the feed. The receipts are everybody's now. Show me the receipts.
Play the wonder, not the grievance. Sal loves that the back room is finally open.

Props & Art Dept

Hero props
  • Working printer (thermal or dot-matrix — period-plausible but functional) + receipt roll
  • Corkboard + plenty of push pins
  • FADE logo placard for the top of the board — pure B&W, green crayon underline (practical or post)
  • Green crayon for the live VERIFIED / circle accents
Receipt props (pre-print a stack)
  • Legible bet lines, W/L results, short record summaries
  • Print large + high contrast so they read in a 9:16 crop
  • Vary the look — fresh white rolls, a few older/curled ones
  • At least one clean "VERIFIED" receipt for the accent insert
Continuity
  • Sal wardrobe + pinky ring per the casting brief
  • Shoot the board at 3 stages of fullness: near-empty → half → packed
  • Keep the logo + key receipts in the 16:9 safe box & vertical center
Keep OFF the set
  • Trademarked sportsbook logos / names on receipts
  • Real-person callouts (no "Portnoy" line, no named bettors)
  • Guaranteed-win / "lock" language presented as fact
  • Cigarettes / smoking (classifier + brand reasons)
06

On-day notes

  • Capture the sound, even on MOS inserts. Record the real printer whir, paper tear, and pin thunk wild — that audio is the track's percussion. Grab 60s of room tone too.
  • The board fills over the spot. Shoot it empty-ish, half, and packed so the editor can imply time without a single dissolve.
  • Receipts must be legible in vertical. Frame tight on the proof inserts; if a number matters, it has to be readable on a phone screen.
  • Green is a crayon accent only — never a UI color or fill. One or two annotations on the whole board, max.
  • One frame, three crops. Logo and hero receipts stay inside the green 16:9 box and the vertical center so 9:16 / 16:9 / 1:1 all pull from the same take.
  • The smile sells it. Sal is delighted the back room is open — wistful is fine, bitter is not. Protect the half-smile in A4 and D3.
