1 · The Idea

We’re capturing real, sensory testimonials from sports fans who bet with Fade. Not polished ad-speak — the kind of vivid, in-the-moment storytelling that makes a viewer feel the matchday before they’ve thought about it.

Every clip should be able to stand alone in the edit: a self-contained, emotive, sensory soundbite about loving sport, backing a team, and the thrill of having skin in the game — building toward the World Cup.

2 · What We’re Capturing

Four themes. Each delivered with concrete, sensory detail — what they see, hear, smell, feel — not just how they feel about it.

  1. Their team — who they support and the bond behind it
  2. Why they love sports — what the game gives them
  3. Why they bet — what backing a wager adds to the experience
  4. World Cup / home nation — pride, nerves, and where they’ll be watching
3 · Tone & Style
  • Sensory and present-tense. “I’m walking up the steps and the pitch opens up in front of me…” Immediate, alive, cinematic.
  • Honest, not salesy. The energy comes from genuine passion, not selling.
  • Range. Capture both the loud, euphoric deliveries and the quiet, half-whispered ones — sensory lines often land better hushed, and it gives the editor options.
  • Self-contained. Talent folds the question into the answer and speaks in full sentences (“My team is…”, not just “Liverpool”) so each clip works on its own.
4 · Prompts by Theme

Open-ended by design — no yes/no answers. Push for the senses every time.

Their team
  • Take me to your seat in the stadium. What do you see, hear, smell?
  • Describe the exact moment your team scores — what happens around you?
  • What does the shirt feel like when you pull it on for matchday?
  • How did you first fall for this team?
Why they love sports
  • Describe the smell of fresh turf and the sound of the crowd before kickoff.
  • What does that first roar feel like in your chest?
  • Paint the scene of your perfect matchday, from the moment you wake up.
  • What does sport give you that nothing else does?
Why they bet
  • Describe the feeling in your stomach in the final minutes when your bet’s still alive.
  • What does the room sound like when it comes through?
  • Walk me through that split second when everything’s on the line.
  • What does having a stake in the game add to watching it?
World Cup / home nation

Every fan must name their home nation, use their team’s nickname, and put their World Cup pride into words. (See the reference in Section 6.)

  • What’s your home nation, and what does it mean to wear those colours?
  • Describe the colours, the flags, the noise when your nation plays.
  • What does it smell and sound like watching with your people?
  • Take me to where you’ll be — the bar, the living room, the street — and make me feel it.
  • Why do you love the World Cup? What does that pride feel like when your nation walks out?

Casting should aim for a spread of nations for variety. Each fan should name their nation, drop the team nickname, and speak to its World Cup pride. Examples below — swap in whatever fits your cast.

“When the Seleção plays, the whole street turns yellow and green — drums going, and you believe, every single time, the trophy’s coming home.”

7 · Directing the Talent
  • Name the sense. People default to “it was amazing” unless prompted directly — ask “what could you smell?”, “what did you hear?”
  • Push for present tense. It forces detail and feels more immediate on screen.
  • Let silences sit. The descriptive gold usually arrives after the first answer — stay quiet and they keep reaching.
  • Roll early. Mic up and start a warm-up chat while recording; the best lines often come before talent thinks we’ve “started.”
  • Get full-sentence takes. Re-ask if an answer starts mid-thought or relies on the question for context.
  • Lock the home-nation beat. Don’t wrap a fan until you have a clean take of their nation, nickname, and World Cup pride.
8 · Session Structure (30–45 min)
First ~5 minMic up + warm-up chat (rolling)
Middle 20–30 minWork the four themes, 2–3 prompts each
Last ~5 minPickups: punchy one-liners + branded tags
9 · Casting Notes
  • Genuine sports fans, or talent who can convincingly carry that passion.
  • A spread of supported teams and home nations for World Cup range (see Section 6).
  • Comfortable improvising and reaching for sensory detail on the spot.
