What we mean

Insider trading, prediction-market style.

On a stock exchange, insider trading means trading on material, non-public information. Kalshi is a U.S. exchange regulated by the CFTC, so that same family of rules applies to it — a line Kalshi draws sharply between itself and offshore platforms. The hard part is that prediction markets stretch the idea into strange new territory.

The strange part

The "inside information" might be a classified military operation, an economic data release, or simply whether a specific person shows up to an event. ABC News framed the open question plainly: how do insider-trading laws built for Wall Street extend to a wager on a celebrity's attendance at an awards show?

Why Kalshi is exposed

Kalshi's markets run on real-world events — elections, government action, sports, pop culture. The more its contracts touch things insiders can know early, the more surface area there is for someone to trade ahead of the public.

The other edge

Separate from illegal insider trading, professional firms can hold speed and information advantages over everyday users. Critics argue the average bettor is effectively up against Wall Street market makers — a structural edge, not a crime, but part of the same "is it level?" question.

On the record

The cases.

A sample of 2026 incidents. Some happened on Kalshi; others happened on the offshore rival Polymarket and are included as context for the category. Being charged or investigated is not the same as being convicted — several of these are ongoing.

01Kalshi

George Santos & the State of the Union

Under federal investigation

The former congressman is reported to have bet on Kalshi that he would not attend the 2026 State of the Union — then publicly said he'd be in the gallery, sending the odds soaring, before no-showing and watching them crash. Kalshi flagged the trades and referred them to the CFTC and DOJ.

Source: ABC News, NPR (Jun 2026)

02Kalshi

Candidates betting on their own races

Fined & suspended by Kalshi

In April 2026, Kalshi fined three congressional candidates and suspended their accounts for five years after finding they had wagered on their own elections. Kalshi now bans sitting members of Congress from holding accounts at all.

Source: CBS News (May 2026)

03Polymarket

The Maduro-raid soldier

Criminal charges — pleaded not guilty

An Army special-forces soldier was charged with using classified knowledge of the operation to capture Nicolás Maduro to win about $400,000 — the first U.S. criminal charges over prediction-market bets. Notably, this was on Polymarket; Kalshi had reportedly blocked him at signup over its ID requirements.

Source: U.S. DOJ, NPR, Reuters (Apr 2026)

04Polymarket

The Google employee

Criminal charges

A Google employee was charged with making more than $1 million by correctly betting on search-trend questions using confidential company information — again on Polymarket, not Kalshi.

Source: NPR (Jun 2026)

05Polymarket

Bets on the Iran operations

Arrests abroad

Israeli authorities arrested several people on suspicion of using classified information to place bets about military operations in Iran — another offshore-platform case that fueled the broader insider-trading alarm.

Source: NPR (Apr 2026)

The pattern

The criminal headlines have mostly hit offshore Polymarket; Kalshi's own record so far is enforcement (Santos referral, candidate suspensions, congressional ban). Whether that reflects cleaner markets or earlier-stage scrutiny is exactly what regulators are now probing.

The defense

What Kalshi says it's doing.

"No. 1 priority"

Kalshi's surveillance lead has called monitoring for insider trading the company's top priority, saying it launched 200+ investigations last year and already surpassed that in the first quarter of 2026 alone.

How it hunts

Kalshi says it scrapes social media, employment records, and FEC campaign-finance data to name-match suspicious traders to the events they bet on, overseen by an independent Surveillance Advisory Committee.

Backing a ban

CEO Tarek Mansour publicly backed a bill from Rep. Ritchie Torres to ban insider trading on prediction markets, arguing Kalshi already enforces similar rules — and drawing a hard line between regulated U.S. Kalshi and offshore or decentralized platforms.

Read it straight: Kalshi's regulated status, active referrals, and self-imposed bans cut in its favor — this is a company publicly leaning into enforcement, not hiding from it. Sources: ABC News, crypto.news (Jan–Feb 2026).
The pressure

The scrutiny.

Congress is looking

In May 2026, House Oversight chair James Comer opened an investigation into both Kalshi and Polymarket, demanding internal documents on how they detect insider trading and verify the identities of domestic and foreign account holders.

Enforcement so far

The CFTC's enforcement chief warned wrongdoers the agency will find them — but as of the reporting had brought only one 2026 prediction-market action (the Maduro soldier), with more said to be "in the pipeline."

The political tangle

Context worth flagging: Donald Trump Jr. is a paid adviser to Kalshi (and an investor/adviser to Polymarket), and Truth Social is launching its own market, Truth Predict — so the industry sits close to political power.

Sources: CBS News, CNBC, PBS NewsHour (May–Jun 2026)

Why it matters to the study.

Kalshi says anybody can win. Insider cases — and a Wall-Street-grade information edge — put a question mark on how level the field really is for the average bettor.

That's the exact question the FADE × Kalshi study is built to test: not to assume the deck is stacked, and not to take the "truth machine" pitch at face value — but to run real bettors, track every result publicly, and let the receipts answer. This brief is the integrity backdrop behind that test.
