The bot

“I built a bot. It pulls every order book, devigs the line to a true no-vig fair value, and flags the mispricing against my model’s implied probability. It backtested beautifully. [beat] That’s the problem. Things that backtest beautifully are overfit. I overfit my way into a forty-thousand-dollar drawdown.”
“The bot’s calibration is immaculate. Brier score in the low teens. When it says sixty percent, it hits sixty percent. I am, by every forecasting metric, an excellent predictor. [beat] I am also broke. Calibration is not profit. Nobody tells you that.”
“My closing line value is positive. Positive CLV. You understand what that means? I’m beating the market price at settlement, which is supposed to predict long-term profit. [beat] It has not. I have positive CLV and negative everything else. I’m a paradox.”

Smarter than the market

“My problem isn’t the edge. The edge is real. My problem is adverse selection — every time I get filled, it’s because someone with sharper information wanted me to. I’m the liquidity. I built a machine to become the liquidity.”
“I run quarter-Kelly. Fractional Kelly sizing, very disciplined, textbook bankroll management. [beat] Quarter of a bad number is still a bad number. The Kelly criterion assumes you have an edge. That’s the load-bearing assumption. We’re auditing it.”
“My Sharpe ratio is negative. Do you know how hard it is to achieve a negative Sharpe with this much conviction? That’s not bad luck. That’s a signal. I’m choosing to ignore the signal.”

Why he’s on Fade

“I got on Fade to benchmark my returns against a real population, not just the market. Now I have an auditable, time-stamped record. Verifiable proof of a negative Sharpe. Most people would hide that. I find the transparency clarifying.”
“Everyone in this space reports returns like it’s a pitch deck — selective, survivorship-biased, no drawdown disclosure. Fade just shows the record. No vig on the truth. Turns out most of these guys are running a story, not a strategy. Myself included. But mine’s at least falsifiable.”
“Ever since my predictions synced to Fade, everyone’s been going against my picks. [beat] And making money.”
“Apparently I’m the most profitable signal on the app. [beat] Inverted. A perfect contrarian indicator is still alpha. It’s just… someone else’s.”
