Revenue · Stream 1

Sportsbook affiliate partnerships.

The primary near-term revenue driver. When a Fade user signs up at a sportsbook through the app, Fade earns a commission — either CPA (a one-time payment per new depositing customer) or revenue share (an ongoing percentage of the sportsbook's net revenue from that customer).

Revenue · Stream 2

Advertising & sponsorships.

Sportsbooks are among the highest-spending digital advertisers in the U.S. Fade offers a highly targeted audience of active bettors — exactly who sportsbooks, DFS platforms, and betting-adjacent brands want to reach. This stream scales with the user base and grows more valuable as engagement climbs.

Ad placement opportunities

In-feed

Sponsored content

Sportsbook promos surfaced naturally in the community feed (e.g. “DraftKings: $200 bonus for new users”). Native feel, strong conversion.

Display

Banner placements

Standard display on analytics screens and leaderboards. Sports-betting CPMs run $15–$40 — well above general display.

Premium

Sponsored tools

A sportsbook sponsors a feature (e.g. “Odds Comparison powered by FanDuel”). Commands $2,000–$10,000/mo depending on user base.

Push

Notification sponsorship

Sponsored alerts for major events (e.g. “Super Bowl props are live on BetMGM”). High open + conversion, premium pricing.

Ad revenue projections

Modest at low user counts, but it scales meaningfully. At 10,000 MAU with a sports-betting audience, expect $3–$5 ARPU from ads alone. Direct sponsorship deals become viable above 5,000 MAU.

Revenue · Stream 3

Premium subscriptions.

A freemium model converts engaged free users into paying subscribers by gating advanced features behind a monthly or annual plan. The free tier is valuable enough to build community; premium is clearly worth the upgrade for anyone who takes betting seriously.
